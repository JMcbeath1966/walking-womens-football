Playing - Things to consider!

Following the sucess of Englands women football team, womens football is enjoying an exponential uptake for women of all ages joining the game.

Very different to the regular game of football, walking football caters primarily for the over 40s, although some tournaments are now running for players exclusively in their 60s and 70s.

Walking football is played at a much slower pace allowing many players who have loved the game throughout their lives to continue playing albeit at a gentler rate, and at the same time encourages players new to the game to take part knowing that the threat of any injury is dramatically reduced.

We do recommend players wear 'breathable' clothing if possible, but anything they feel comfortable in is fine. A shirt or short designed specifically for sports allows body heat to be expelled and air to circulate better around the body, keeping players cool and preventing overheating.

It doesn't have to be expensive if you want to invest in kits. The only thing you may need to buy that you donâ€™t already own is a pair of shin pads and perhaps some appropriate footwear.

Walking Football can be played in any kind of training shoe, but 'football-specific' trainers will give you more stability and better ball control. The venue may specify what types of footwear are allowed/not allowed. You are advised to contact the club/sports centre before attending.

However, if you begin to play Walking Football competitively, you will need to wear the appropriate kit.

We recommend:

  • Surface appropriate trainers or boots
  • A breathable shirt
  • Breathable shorts
  • Shin Pads
  • Hat, cap and gloves - as appropriate for the weather
